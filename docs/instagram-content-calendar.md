**Day 3 — Education**
Caption:
Most freelance proposals lose before the client finishes reading them.

The mistake: leading with your services, your experience, your process.

The fix: lead with their problem.

When a client reads their own situation described accurately, they lean forward. That's the moment you have their attention.

Structure that converts:
1. Their problem (1 paragraph)
2. Your approach for THIS project specifically
3. Scope — what's in, what's explicitly NOT in
4. Three-tier pricing (not one number)
5. Timeline + one obvious next step

One number = yes or no.
Three options = which one.

#freelanceproposal #freelancetips #clientwork #consultant

**Day 6 — Education**
Caption:
The freelance contract clause most people skip:

The **approval clause**.

Without it: client approves work, receives files, pays invoice, and comes back 6 weeks later asking for "a few changes."

With it: "Work is considered approved upon written sign-off, or after 5 business days of silence following delivery. Changes after approval are billed as new work."

The silence clause saves you from projects that never actually close.

Add it to every contract.

**Day 17 — Education**
Caption:
How to raise your freelance rates without torpedoing client relationships:

1. Test new rates on new clients first — never existing ones
2. Raise 15-25% at a time, not in one big jump
3. Give 30-60 days notice to existing clients
4. Keep the message short. Don't apologize. Don't over-explain.
5. Expect some pushback. First pushback is usually a negotiation reflex, not a hard ceiling.

The clients worth keeping will stay.
The ones who leave over a fair rate increase were going to be problems anyway.

#raisingyourrates #freelancepricing #freelancetips #freelancebusiness

The quarterly freelance audit:

Every 90 days, answer these 4 questions:

1. What was my effective hourly rate across all projects this quarter?
2. Which clients produced the most revenue per hour spent?
3. Which clients cost me more time than they paid for?
4. If I could change one thing about how I work next quarter, what would it be?

Most freelancers never run the numbers. The ones who do consistently raise their rates and improve their client mix.

30 minutes. Do it this week.
