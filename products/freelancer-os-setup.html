Quick Start (15 Minutes)

1

Duplicate the template to your Notion workspace

Open the link from your receipt. You must have a Notion account (free plan works). Click "Duplicate" in the top right corner. Choose your workspace. Done — it's yours permanently.

2

Add your business details to the Home page

Open the "🏠 Home" page. Update: your name, business name, current monthly revenue goal, and 3 active priorities. This page is your weekly anchor — you'll open it every Monday morning.

3

Add your current clients to the Client CRM

Open "👥 Clients." Add one row per client. Fill in: name, company, email, status (Active), and source (how you found them). You'll link projects and invoices to these records later.

4

Add your active projects to the Project Hub

Open "📁 Projects." Add one row per active project. Link each to the relevant client record. Set status to "In Progress." Add the project deadline and contract value.

5

Log outstanding invoices in the Invoice Tracker

Open "🧾 Invoices." Add any sent-but-unpaid invoices. Link to client. Set status to "Sent" or "Overdue." The revenue dashboard updates automatically.

6

Bookmark the "📅 Today" view as your daily driver

The Today view shows tasks due today, filtered from all projects. Open this first every morning. Add it to your browser bookmarks. This is where you work from, not from the full project list.

⚡ Setup shortcut:

Don't try to fill in everything perfectly on day one. Get clients, projects, and invoices in. The rest fills in naturally over the first week of use. A 70% filled-in system you actually use beats a perfect one you don't touch.

👤 Client Portal (per project)

A shareable Notion page you create for each active client. They see progress in real-time without needing Notion access.

How to set up for each client:

  1. Duplicate the "Client Portal Template" page from the Templates section
  2. Rename it to "ClientName — Project Portal"
  3. Add a filtered view of tasks linked to this project (filter: Project = this project; Status ≠ Done)
  4. Share to web: click Share → Share to web → toggle "Allow comments"
  5. Send the link to your client — no Notion account required to view

Views to Use Daily

📅 Today View (open every morning)

Tasks filtered to: Due Date = Today, Status ≠ Done. Sorted by Priority. This is your daily task list — the only view you need during the workday.

💰 Revenue Dashboard (check weekly)

Invoices grouped by Status. Shows: Paid This Month (sum), Outstanding Total (sum), and Overdue count. Update invoice statuses when payments arrive.

🔭 Pipeline (check twice a week)

Client CRM filtered to Status = Prospect, sorted by Last Contact (ascending). Shows who you haven't talked to recently. Move fast on stale prospects.

📋 Active Projects Board (check weekly)

Projects filtered to Status = In Progress, displayed as a kanban by status. Quickly see which projects are moving and which are stuck.

⚠️ Most common setup mistake:

Not linking tasks to projects. Unlinked tasks are orphans — they don't appear in project views, don't contribute to hours-logged totals, and make the Today view useless. Always link tasks to a project when you create them.

Weekly Routine (takes 20 minutes)

M

Monday: Plan your week

Open the Active Projects board. For each active project: add tasks due this week with specific due dates. Review the Pipeline — anyone who needs a follow-up?

F

Friday: Weekly review

Open the Weekly Review template (duplicate from Templates section). Answer 5 questions: What got done? What didn't? Any red flags? Revenue check (any overdue invoices)? What's the #1 priority next week? File it. Takes 15 minutes. Compounds significantly over time.
