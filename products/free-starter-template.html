Setup Guide

Set up in 15 minutes

1

Duplicate the template

Click "Duplicate" in the top-right corner of the Notion page. Select your workspace. The template copies over with all databases, views, and example data intact.

2

Delete the example data

Each database comes with 2–3 example entries to show you the structure. Delete these once you've seen how they work. Start fresh with your own clients and projects.

3

Add your current clients and projects

Add one row to the Client CRM for each active or recent client. Move each to the appropriate status (Prospect, Active, or Complete). Then add your current active project for each client.

4

Log any outstanding invoices

Add one row to the Invoice Tracker for any unpaid invoices you currently have outstanding. Mark their status (Sent, Overdue, or Paid). This immediately shows you what you're waiting on.

5

Bookmark your dashboard

Bookmark the main workspace page in your browser. Put it in your bookmarks bar. This only works if you open it every morning — the habit is the system.

Daily Workflow

How to use this in 10 minutes a day

Every morning (5 min)

  1. Open the Task Manager "Today" view. What are you doing today?
  2. Open the Client CRM. Does anyone have a follow-up due today?
  3. Check the Invoice Tracker. Any invoices in the "Overdue" group?

When you finish work (2 min)

  1. Mark completed tasks as Done in the Task Manager
  2. Add any new tasks that came up during the day
  3. Update any Client CRM entries that changed (e.g., new last-contact date)

When you send an invoice (2 min)

  1. Add a new row in the Invoice Tracker
  2. Set status to "Sent" and set the due date
  3. Update the client's row in the CRM with the invoice amount
Weekly Ritual

The 15-minute Friday review

This is the single habit that separates freelancers who feel in control from those who feel reactive. Schedule 15 minutes every Friday and answer these five questions.

Weekly Review Template

Copy this to a new Notion page each Friday. Answer each question honestly — this becomes your business journal over time.

1. What did I complete this week? (List actual deliverables shipped)
2. What's stalled, and why? (Projects waiting on a client, a decision, or me)
3. Revenue check: What did I invoice? What's outstanding? What's overdue?
4. Any red flags? (Scope creep, unhappy clients, late payments, under-capacity)
5. What's the single most important thing I need to do next week?

Do this every Friday for three months. Your freelance practice will be unrecognizable from where it started — not because the questions are magic, but because consistently asking them forces clarity and action that most freelancers never have.
