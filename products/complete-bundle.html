Getting Started

How to set everything up in one afternoon

1

Set up Freelancer OS first (30–45 min)

Open the Freelancer OS file. Click "Duplicate" in Notion's top right. Select your workspace. Delete the example data and add your 3–5 current clients and any active projects. This becomes your daily hub.

2

Save the Client Onboarding documents to Google Drive (10 min)

Open the Client Onboarding Bundle file. Download the proposal and contract templates. Make a copy in Google Drive ("FreelanceKit/Templates"). You'll duplicate these for each new client — never start from scratch again.

3

Bookmark the AI Prompt Pack (5 min)

Open the AI Prompt Pack in your browser. Bookmark it. Keep it in your bookmarks bar next to your other daily tools. Before you write any client email or document, check here first.

4

Run your first weekly review (15 min)

This Friday, open Freelancer OS and go through the weekly review questions. Answer them honestly. Do this every Friday for 8 weeks. Your freelance practice will look completely different by then — not because anything is magic, but because clarity and consistency compound.

Important

These tools only work if you actually use them. The biggest predictor of success isn't which tools you have — it's whether you open them consistently. Put Freelancer OS in your browser's bookmarks bar. Add a Friday calendar event for the weekly review. Use the AI prompts before you write anything substantial. Make it automatic, not aspirational.
